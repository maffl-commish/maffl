Lower-Tier

    • MAFFL will consist of 2 espn.com leagues and player pools, the “MAFFL Upper-Tier” league and the “MAFFL Lower-Tier” league.
    • Seasonal Entry Fee and Prizes, along with the new Credit system, are common to all MAFFL owners regardless of league placement.
    • The 2 leagues will have an identical rule set, the only exceptions being league size, divisions and post-season play.
    • Each season, 3 teams from the Upper-Tier will be relegated and 3 teams from the Lower-Tier will be promoted (see Promotion/Relegation Overview).
    • In-Season game rules will adhere to the ESPN League Settings (ESPN.com).
    • Following the 2024 placements, all new owners will enter in the Lower-Tier league.
    • We will fill unexpected Upper-Tier league openings with the highest Credit total from the Lower-Tier league (tiebreaker: the highest ranking team from the previous season).
    • The commissioner has final say on any disputes but will consider league votes for fairness.

    Each season, 3 teams move down from the Upper-Tier and 3 teams move up from the Lower-Tier. Final regular-season standings set each team’s post-season path; the tables below show the full finish-by-finish matrix.

    How the 3rd spots are decided

    In each tier, two of the three moves are automatic and the third is settled head-to-head. Lower-Tier finishes 1–2 are promoted automatically; Upper-Tier finishes 11–12 are relegated automatically (they still play a consolation 3-game series against each other). The remaining promotion and relegation spots are decided by the Upper-Tier Relegation Showdown (#9 vs #10) and the Lower-Tier Promotion Showdown (#3 vs #4), each a 3-game series across NFL Wks 15–17.

    How Divisions Are Set

    Each offseason, Upper-Tier division alignment is set by credit totals: owners are seeded into the four divisions (A–D) by their credit rank. Alignment locks on August 1, after which owners may spend credits on a Division Swap (see Credit System) during the Aug 1 → Labor Day window. The Lower-Tier has no divisions.

    Alignment Formula

    Credit-rank groupings fill the four divisions on the pattern 1/6/12 · 2/5/11 · 3/4/10 · 7/8/9 (Divisions A–D). Credit ties are broken by previous-season W/L %, then previous-season total points.

    The current season’s groupings are posted under 2026 Upper-Tier Division Alignment on the Info tab. Full year-by-year division history lives on the History page.

    • Entry Fee: Each team pays $100 per season to participate.
    • Total Prize Pool: Calculated as $100 × total number of teams across both leagues.
    • Prize Allocation:
      • 67% (2/3) of the total prize pool goes to the Upper-Tier League.
      • 33% (1/3) of the total prize pool goes to the Lower-Tier League.

    Example Calculation

    If there are 12 teams in the Upper-Tier and 8 teams in the Lower-Tier:

    • Total Prize Pool: $100 × (12 + 8) = $2,000
    • Upper-Tier Pool (67%) = $1,340
    • Lower-Tier Pool (33%) = $660
    • Champion of Lower-Tier would receive $330 (50% of $660)
    • Division Winner in Upper-Tier would receive $50.25 in prizes (15% of $1,340, divided by 4)

    Overview

    • The credit system creates a new league currency, issued to owners based on each team’s performance over a season and across multiple seasons.
    • Credits are awarded for both overall season outcomes and specific in-season achievements (bonus points).
    • The credit system does not directly affect promotion or relegation.
    • Both Upper-Tier and Lower-Tier are rewarded on a similar scale, ensuring fairness regardless of league level.
    • Credit balance carries weight, and earned credits can be spent for competitive perks.

    Rules

    • The Credits page serves as the source of truth when utilizing the Credit Balance for tiebreaking purposes.
    • All Credit transactions are logged on the Credits page.
    • All credits will be awarded at the end of the season.
    • Each season’s total (Outcome Credits + Bonus Credits) is added to a running cumulative credits total.
    • All credit transactions require commissioner approval.

    Impact

    • Credit Balance serves as the tiebreaker for all division, playoff and end-of-season seeding.
    • Determines annual division alignment for the Upper-Tier League (1/6/12; 2/5/11; 3/4/10; 7/8/9). Tiebreakers determined by previous season W/L%, then total points.
    • Determines Upper-Tier placement if unexpected openings occur.
    • Credits are earned and can be spent (see options below).
    • Credits can be traded directly or packaged with players. (These trades adhere to the normal trade policy and deadlines.)

    Lower-Tier Odd-Team RuleNEW·2026

    • MAFFL Ghost exists to solve the bye-week problem: a 14-week regular season cannot schedule an odd number of teams evenly (the lone exception being a 15-team league, where everyone plays each other exactly once).
    • Applies only when the Lower-Tier has an odd number of teams. Upper-Tier is fixed at 12 teams and is unaffected.
    • A placeholder team called “MAFFL Ghost” is added to the Lower-Tier ESPN league to create an even schedule. MAFFL Ghost carries no roster.
    • Each week, the commissioner drops the single highest real-team score, totals the remaining real teams’ scores, divides by that remaining count, and applies the result to MAFFL Ghost as a scoring adjustment in ESPN LM Tools.
    • The real team matched up against MAFFL Ghost that week wins if they beat the average and loses if they don’t.
    • MAFFL Ghost does not earn credits, win prizes, or affect promotion/relegation.
    • All real teams remain eligible for weekly high score prizes every week, including the team facing MAFFL Ghost.
    • On Draft Day, MAFFL Ghost “drafts” a full, non-consequential 22-player roster like any other team; that roster is abruptly dropped to the waiver wire immediately following the draft.
    Example

    Nine Lower-Tier teams play in a given week. The single highest score is dropped, leaving eight teams that scored a combined 920 points. MAFFL Ghost score = 920 / 8 = 115.0. The team facing MAFFL Ghost that scored 119 wins. A team that scored 108 would have lost.
