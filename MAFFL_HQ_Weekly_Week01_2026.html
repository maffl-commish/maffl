    MAFFL runs two ESPN.com leagues and player pools: the Upper-Tier league and the Lower-Tier league. Entry fee, prizes, and the Credit system are common to all MAFFL owners regardless of placement.

    Both leagues run an identical rule set — the only exceptions are league size, divisions, and post-season play. Each season, 3 teams are relegated from Upper-Tier and 3 teams are promoted from Lower-Tier.

    All new owners enter through the Lower-Tier. Unexpected Upper-Tier openings are filled by the Lower-Tier owner with the highest Credit total (tiebreaker: highest previous-season finish).

    In-season game rules follow ESPN League Settings. The commissioner has final say on disputes but will consider league votes for fairness.

    Each team pays $100 per season. The total prize pool equals $100 × total teams across both leagues. The pool splits 67% Upper / 33% Lower.

    Upper-Tier Distribution (67%)
    • Champion — 50% of Upper pool
    • Runner-Up — 15%
    • Division Winners — 15% (split 4 ways)
    • Weekly High Score Bonus — 20% (split across 14 payouts)
    Lower-Tier Distribution (33%)
    • Champion — 50% of Lower pool
    • Runner-Up — 15%
    • Promotion Showdown Winner — 15%
    • Weekly High Score Bonus — 20% (split across 14 payouts)
    Example (12 Upper + 8 Lower)

    Pool: $100 × 20 = $2,000. Upper pool = $1,340. Lower pool = $660. Lower Champion takes $330 (50% of $660). Each Upper Division Winner gets $50.25 (15% of $1,340 ÷ 4).
